This platform showcases our full systems thinking analysis on last-mile connectivity in Indian cities.

Through causal loop diagrams, system archetypes, and leverage point analysis, we aim to uncover root causes and propose impactful interventions to improve urban mobility for all.

Problem Statement

In many Indian cities, the lack of reliable and accessible last-mile connectivity continues to severely hinder the effectiveness and adoption of metro and public transport systems. While metros and buses efficiently cover major routes, the absence of seamless, safe, and affordable transport options from stations to commuters' final destinations discourages daily use. This critical gap leads to increased dependence on private vehicles, traffic congestion, and underutilization of costly public transport infrastructure. Without addressing last-mile challenges, the broader goals of urban mobility, sustainability, and inclusive access remain out of reach.

EPS Analysis

Event → Pattern → Structure

Event (Surface Symptoms)

  • Poor accessibility to metro stations from residential areas.
  • Low adoption of public transport despite well-built metro infrastructure.
  • Increase in private vehicle usage and traffic congestion.
  • Longer and unpredictable commute times.
  • Safety concerns and discomfort in last-mile travel, especially for women and the elderly.

Pattern (Trends Over Time)

  • Commuters avoid metros due to inconvenience
  • Rising preference for private vehicles
  • Reliance on informal or unreliable last-mile transport options.

Structure (Deep Systemic Causes)

  • Lack of planning between agencies.
  • Absence of real-time data systems to monitor last-mile issues.
  • No integration policy
  • Cultural preferences and beliefs around the convenience of private vehicles.
  • Gaps in regulatory enforcement around transport quality and safety.

Community Awareness Campaigns Mindset/Paradigms (#2) Shifts perception from private vehicle dependency to public/shared transport. Long-term behavioral change, improves civic participation.
Urban Planning Reforms Goals of the System (#3) Encourages transit-oriented development around metro stations. Better accessibility, walkability, and infrastructure.

System Archetypes

Shifting the Burden

Heavy reliance on private vehicles and ride-hailing apps as a short-term fix for poor last-mile solutions. Structural improvements (infrastructure, integration) are ignored.

Fixes that Fail

Adding more autos without managing parking zones increases congestion, making the situation worse over time.
